## 7. Full page content

### A simple way to think about it

Imagine you're trying to drop a coin into a small slot — eyes open vs eyes closed. Both can work; one is much more reliable.

That's the difference between a **landmark ("blind") injection** and an **ultrasound-guided injection**.

> Read the technical details on the [Ultrasound-Guided Orthopedic Injections](/ultrasound-guided-orthopedic-injections) pillar.

---

### The 8 biggest benefits

#### 1. Higher accuracy
The needle is delivered exactly into the joint, tendon sheath, or bursa under live imaging. No guessing.

#### 2. Greater safety
Vessels and nerves are visualised and avoided. The needle path is planned around delicate structures.

#### 3. More predictable pain relief
When the medication actually reaches the right tissue, outcomes improve — especially in tendons and small joints.

#### 4. Fewer wasted injections
Repeat or "re-do" injections become less frequent because the first one was placed correctly.

#### 5. Diagnostic clarity
The same scan that guides the injection also helps confirm the diagnosis — tendon tears, fluid collections, bursitis can be seen live.

#### 6. Better experience
Many patients describe ultrasound-guided injections as more comfortable because the needle path is precise rather than exploratory.

#### 7. No radiation
Ultrasound uses sound waves, not X-rays. Safe for repeat use, including in younger and reproductive-age patients.

#### 8. Especially valuable for difficult anatomy
Larger body habitus, prior surgery, deep targets (hip), or unusual anatomy — image guidance handles them well.

---

### When does precision matter most?

- **Shoulder injections** — small bursae, rotator cuff, biceps sheath, AC joint
- **Hip joint injections** — deep target
- **Tendon injections** (peritendinous) — close to neurovascular structures
- **GFC and HA injections** — high-value injectate that should not be wasted in surrounding tissue
- **Patients who didn't respond to prior blind injections**

---

### When is landmark technique still acceptable?

For some straightforward, large-target injections (e.g., a clearly accessible knee joint in a slim patient with no swelling), landmark technique can be acceptable in experienced hands. We will be transparent about which approach we recommend for *you*.

## 8. FAQ section

**Q1. Are ultrasound-guided injections always better?**
For tendons, small targets, and deep joints — yes, in most cases. For some straightforward injections, landmark technique can still be acceptable.

**Q2. Are image-guided injections more painful?**
Usually no. Many patients find them more comfortable because the path is precise.

**Q3. Is there extra cost?**
There is often a small additional cost for the imaging component. Many patients find the improved outcome worth it.

**Q4. Is the procedure longer?**
Typically only a few minutes longer than a landmark injection.

**Q5. Can I have repeat ultrasound-guided injections safely?**
Yes — there is no radiation involved.

**Q6. Will I see the inside of my own joint?**
Often yes — your doctor can show you the live image during the visit.

**Q7. Does insurance cover ultrasound guidance?**
Coverage varies. The clinic team will help you check.

**Q8. Can pregnant women have these injections?**
Certain non-medication procedures may be considered with obstetric coordination; case-by-case.

**Q9. Does ultrasound replace MRI?**
For many soft-tissue diagnoses, ultrasound is highly informative. MRI is added when deeper or structural detail is needed.
