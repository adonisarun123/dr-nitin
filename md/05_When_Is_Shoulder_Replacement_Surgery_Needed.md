### What Is Shoulder Replacement Surgery?

Shoulder replacement surgery involves replacing the **damaged parts of the shoulder joint** — typically the ball (humeral head) and sometimes the socket (glenoid) — with **artificial components** made of metal and plastic.

This procedure helps:

*   Reduce chronic pain
*   Restore mobility
*   Improve overall shoulder function
*   Enhance quality of life

### When Is It Recommended?

Shoulder replacement surgery is typically recommended when **conservative treatments are no longer effective** and joint damage is advanced.

#### Common Conditions That May Require Surgery:

*   **Severe Osteoarthritis or Rheumatoid Arthritis**: Cartilage breakdown leads to bone-on-bone friction and joint stiffness.
*   **Irreparable Rotator Cuff Injuries**: Chronic or massive tears that cause instability and pain.
*   **Complex Shoulder Fractures**: Especially in older adults, when broken bones are hard to align or heal properly.
*   **Failed Previous Shoulder Surgeries**: Revision surgery may be needed if earlier procedures haven’t restored function.

### What to Expect from the Procedure

Shoulder replacement is a safe and effective surgical solution, especially when performed by an experienced orthopaedic specialist.

During the surgery:

*   The **damaged bone and cartilage** are carefully removed.
*   Artificial components are implanted to **mimic natural joint movement**.
*   Post-surgical recovery includes **physiotherapy** to regain strength and range of motion.

Most patients experience **significant pain relief and improved shoulder function** within a few months of surgery.
