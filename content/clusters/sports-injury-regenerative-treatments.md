### Why sports injuries deserve a smarter approach

Sports injuries — even "minor" ones — alter your tissue, your training, and sometimes your motivation. The wrong approach (too little care, too much rest, or repeated steroid shots) can prolong the problem. The right approach speeds recovery and reduces the chance of recurrence.

## 8. FAQ section

**Q1. Are regenerative injections proven to help sports injuries?**
For many tendinopathies and select joint conditions, yes — when paired with structured rehab and used in appropriate cases.

**Q2. Will GFC slow my training?**
Most athletes return to light activity within 24–72 hours and to full training over 2–6 weeks, depending on the injury.

**Q3. Is PRP or GFC better for me?**
It depends on the injury and your history. Many athletes today are opting for GFC. ➡ [PRP vs GFC](/prp-vs-gfc).

**Q4. Can I have an injection on competition day?**
No. Plan well ahead of an event.

**Q5. Are NSAIDs OK during regenerative recovery?**
We usually advise avoiding NSAIDs for a brief period to support the regenerative response. Paracetamol is usually fine.

**Q6. Will rehab still be needed?**
Yes — and it is the most important part of long-term outcome.

**Q7. Can I run with patellar tendinopathy?**
Often a modified, structured plan allows continued running. Specialist guidance helps.

**Q8. How accurate is ultrasound for diagnosis?**
Highly accurate for tendons, bursae, and many soft-tissue conditions.

**Q9. When is surgery the right answer?**
Full-thickness tears, mechanical blocks, and selected structural problems may benefit from surgery. Honest assessment guides the choice.
